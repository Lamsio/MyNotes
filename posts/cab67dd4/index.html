What is a Project

Project: A temporary endeavor was undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.

Operation: A work done to sustain the on-going business and/or to produce repetitive products and services.

A project ends when its objective accomplish

Why starts a project?

  1. Market demand
  2. Customer request
  3. Legal requirement
  4. Technological advance

When to end a project?

  1. Accomplish objective
  2. Can’t accomplish objective
  3. objective doesn’t exists.

What is PM?

PM: Apply knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements

Project Management Overview
  • Initiating
    1. Identifying important stakeholders
    2. Defining and authorizing a project
  • Planning
    1. design a workable plan to ensure the project meet the organizational needs.
  • Executing
    1. Coordinating people and other resources to carry out the various plans and create the product, services or result of the project
  • Controling & Monitoring
    1. Regularly measuring and monitoring the progress to ensure that project team meets the project objectives.
  • Closing
Ten PM Knowledge areas
  1. Scope
  2. Cost
  3. Time
  4. Quality
  5. Resources
  6. Stakeholder
  7. Risk
  8. Procurement
  9. Communications
  10. Integration

Take the least time to accomplish the objective of stakeholders.

Skills for Project Managers
  1. Communication Skills
  2. Team-building skills
  3. leadership skills
  4. coping skills
  5. organizational skills
  6. technical skills

Project management office(PMO)

PMO: An organization responsible for coordinating project management functional

Project Success Factors

  1. User involving
  2. Executive support
  3. clear business objective
  4. Alige process
  5. Execution
